Fair Process (80913-JA-¶2701)

    Amend by addition to ¶2701 after the list of fair process and renumber accordingly:  
   Complainants' Rights in Fair Process.--General Provisions.--Those bringing complaints are to receive respectful, balanced treatment throughout any grievance and trial procedures.  They shall receive comparable rights to those provided for the respondent.  They shall receive reasonable response from church officers.  Church officers shall seek to build reconciliation among all parties at interest, so long as these efforts are not used to hinder fair process.
    1. In any judicial proceeding, the complainant shall have a right to be heard before any final action is taken.
    2. Notice of any hearing shall advise the complainant about proposed procedures with sufficient detail to allow the complainant to prepare.  Notice shall be given not less than twenty (20) days prior to the hearing.
    3. Complainants shall have the right to be accompanied by another person to any interview or hearing to which they are subject.  The person accompanying them shall have the right to voice.  
    4. Under no circumstances shall one party or counsel, in the absence of the other party or counsel, discuss substantive matters with the members of the judicial hearing or appellate body while the case is pending.  Questions of procedure may be
raised with the presiding officer or secretary of the hearing or appellate body.
    5. The complainant shall have the right to an answer to each question, allegation, specification, or other matter brought.  Officials must provide grounds for their decisions, which are also to be shared with the complainant and respondent.
    6. Confidentiality of the name and accusations of the complainant shall be maintained throughout the proceedings, except from the respondent and his/her counsel, church officers identified by the Discipline,  hearing body members involved, and those present for an open trial.
    7. In the event that a complainant fails to appear for supervisory interviews, refuses mail, refuses to communicate personally with the bishop or district superintendent, or otherwise fails to respond to supervisory requests or requests from official judicial committees or Church Counsel, such actions or inactions shall end that church process, because such processes shall not continue without the participation of the complainant.
      
   In addition, the addition needs to be made in one other paragraph of the Book of Discipline, Paragraph 362.3.

Rationale

Justice and grace requires fairness and care to all sides.  The constitutional right of the accused to face the accuser must be maintained.  And the rights to privacy and to seek redress must not be denied.

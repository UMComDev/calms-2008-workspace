Chargeable Offenses (80840-JA-¶2702.1)

Amend ¶2702.1 Chargeable Offenses and the Statute of Limitations as follows:
A bishop, clergy member of an annual conference (paragraph 368), local pastor (footnote), clergy on honorable or administrative location, or diaconal minister may be tried when charged (subject to the statue of limitations in paragraph 2702.4) with one or more of the following offenses: (a) immorality including but not limited to, not being celibate in singleness or not being faithful in a heterosexual marriage ; (b) practices declared by The United Methodist Church to be incompatible with Christian teachings, (footnote) including but not limited to: being a self avowed practicing homosexual; or conducting ceremonies which celebrate homosexual unions; or performing same-sex wedding ceremonies; (c)  (b)crime; (d)  (c) failure to perform the work of ministry; (e)  (d) disobedience to the Order and Discipline of The United Methodist Church; (f)  (e) dissemination of doctrines contrary to the established standards of doctrine of The United Methodist Church; (g)  (f) relationships and/or behavior that undermines the ministry of another pastor; (footnote) (h)  (g) child abuse; (i)  (h) sexual abuse; (footnote) (j)  (i) sexual misconduct or harassment; or (l)  (j) racial or gender discrimination.  

Rationale

UM tradition is not confessional, creedal, or moralistic; 2004 Discipline encourages using medical, theological, and social science disciplines to understand human sexuality; recent medical science studies indicate genetic and biochemical factors play major role in determining sexual orientation; Biblical scholars, theologians, social scientists, psychologists, and psychiatrists remain divided on homosexuality.

Election of Ordained Ministerial Delegates (80007-MH-¶35-C)

Amend ¶35. Article IV as follows:
¶35. Article IV. – The ordained ministerial delegates to the General Conference and to the Jurisdictional or Central Conference shall be elected from the ordained ministerial members in full connection with the annual conference or provisional annual conference by and from the ordained ministerial members in full connection, the associate members, and local pastors who have completed the course of study.
  
Also Amend ¶316.5 as follows for purposes of consistency:  
The membership of local pastors under full and part-time appointment is in the annual conference where they shall have the right to vote on all matters except constitutional amendments, election of delegates to the General, jurisdictional, or central conferences, and matters of ordination, character, and conference relations of clergy.
  
Also Amend ¶318.5 as follows for purposes of consistency:
¶318.5 Local pastors, except student local pastors, may serve on any board, commission, or committee with voice and vote, except on matters of clergy character, qualifications, status, and ordination, except the board of ordained ministry and the district committee on ordained ministry (634.1). They shall not be eligible to vote on or serve as delegates to the General, jurisdictional, or central conferences.
  
Also Amend ¶602.1.d as follows for purposes of consistency:
Full and part-time local pastors under appointment to a pastoral charge shall have the right to vote in the annual conference on all matters except constitutional amendments, election of delegates to the General and jurisdictional or central conferences , and matters of ordination, character, and conference relations of clergy.

Rationale

There is a growing percentage of our churches served by licensed local pastors and associate members. 28.9% of United Methodist clergy are licensed local pastors and associate members, many of whom are appointed to rural or small membership churches.

Equal Rights (80841-C2-¶162.H)

Amend ¶162.III H Equal Rights Regardless of Sexual Orientation as follows:
Certain basic human rights and civil liberties are due all persons. We are committed to supporting those rights and liberties for homosexual persons  all persons regardless of sexual identity or sexual orientation. We see a clear issue of simple justice in protecting their rightful claims where they have shared material resources, pensions, guardian relationships, mutual powers of attorney, and other such lawful claims typically attendant to contractual relationships that involve shared protection before the law. Moreover, we support efforts to stop violence, marginalization, and other forms of coercion against gays and lesbians  persons based on sexual identity and sexual orientation.  We also commit ourselves to social witness against the  violence, coercion and marginalization of former homosexuals  persons who have transformed their sexual identity and sexual orientation.  

Rationale

UM tradition is not confessional, creedal, or moralistic; 2004 Discipline encourages using medical, theological, and social science disciplines to understand human sexuality; recent medical science studies indicate genetic and biochemical factors play major role in determining sexual orientation; Biblical scholars, theologians, social scientists, psychologists, and psychiatrists remain divided on homosexuality.

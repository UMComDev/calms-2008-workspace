Declaratory Decisions (80942-JA-¶2610.1)

Amend ¶2610.1 Declaratory Decisions as follows:
1. The Judicial Council, on petition as hereinafter provided, shall have jurisdiction to make a ruling in the nature of a declaratory decision as to the constitutionality, meaning, application, or effect of the Discipline or any portion thereof or of any act or legislation of a General Conference; and the decision of the Judicial Council thereon shall be as binding and effectual as a decision made by it on appeal.8  Declaratory decisions by nature may be hypothetical since the Discipline may need to be clarified before it can be applied.  Similarly, if there is a mistake made in applying the Discipline, the Judicial Council may need to be the last resort in settling the difference of interpretation of the passage(s) for “constitutionality, meaning, application, or effect.”

Rationale

From  JCD 189: “The 1944 General Conference Act . . . contained this injunction: "Moot and hypothetical questions will not be decided, but only those where some action is desired, and some doubt or question as to the meaning or application of the Act, legislation, action or ruling is apparent."

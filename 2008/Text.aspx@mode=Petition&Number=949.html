Trial Pool (80949-JA-¶2713.3a)

Amend 2713.3a) as follows:
  
3. a) The trial for a clergy member or a local pastor shall be convened as provided in ¶ 2709 with the pool of thirty-five or more persons to consist of clergy in full connection. If there are not enough persons in appropriate categories in an annual conference to complete the pool, additional persons may be appointed from other annual conferences. All appointments to the pool shall be made by the district superintendents lot, the names drawn randomly in the presence of the counsels for the church and the respondent to assure that all appointees fit the disciplinary requirements.  Special consideration should be given so that the pool includes persons representative of racial, ethnic, and gender diversity.   
    [In addition, the correction needs to be made in other paragraphs of the Book of Discipline, including  ¶ ¶2712.3 and 2714.3]

Rationale

The superintendents participate early in the judicial process by either forwarding or writing the complaints that end up in a church trial.  Under the current Discipline, allowing the superintendents to pick the trial court pool puts them in the position of being complainant and selector of the jury!

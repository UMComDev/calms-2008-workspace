Conflicts of Interest (80848-JA-¶2600)

Add a new paragraph between paragraphs 2603 and 2604 of the 2004 Book of Discipline:
  
      New ¶ Conflicts of Interest—
  1.             Members of the Judicial Council shall recuse themselves from any hearing or deliberation where there is a known conflict of interest.  
        2.    A conflict of interest is defined as any hearing, appeal, decision, or petition involving a person or persons with whom a Judicial Council member has a personal relationship.  
3.    A conflict of interest is further defined as any hearing, appeal, decision, or petition originating from an episcopal area where a member of the Judicial Council has held church membership or served under appointment in any of the previous four years.  
4.    Conflicts of interest shall be identified, whenever possible, prior to the convening of a Judicial Council session so appropriate arrangements may be made for the temporary seating of lay and/or clergy alternates to preserve a quorum.
        

Rationale

There is a long-standing precedent in the USA that judges step aside from cases where there is a known conflict of interest; and  in the Book of Discipline there are no provisions for members of the Judicial Council to recuse themselves from cases that pose a clear conflict of interest.

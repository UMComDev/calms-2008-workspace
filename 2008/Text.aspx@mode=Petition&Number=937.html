Call for Consistent Theology (80937-FO-R9999)

    Add a new resolution as follows:
Whereas there has been a call to the denomination to take a stand on its theology,  
   Whereas that call has been to be faithful to Scripture and that application of Scripture has been almost exclusively identified as the grounds to remove practicing homosexuals from membership and leadership in our church,
    Whereas this call has come in the form of legislation for General Conference vote in lieu of other forms of decision-making (how can this be resolved otherwise?),
    And whereas we need to go back and identify our spiritual roots to withstand this attempt to divide our church with an issue that requires study rather than prejudice,  
    Therefore, be it resolved that the General Conference of the United Methodist Church affirm its General Rules (Do no harm, do good, and participate in the church.   [See Paragraph 103]) and the Wesley Quadrilateral (taking the content of our faith from the Scriptures, from tradition, from reason, and from experience.  [See Paragraph 104]); and,
   Be it further resolved that the General Conference affirm the Arminian nature of our theology (taken by John Wesley from the Dutch theologian Jacob Arminius who emphasized Paul’s Philippians 2:12 teaching of “Work out your own salvation with fear and trembling” as a counter to “Sola Scriptura” and “Sola Fide” of other Protestant Reformers and led Wesley to his emphasis on sanctification where he did not use “Are you saved?” but rather “Are you going on to perfection?”); and,
   Be it further resolved that United Methodists be “known by their fruits” (Matthew 7:15-21), “Do unto others as you would have them do unto you” (Matthew 7:12), “Love God and your neighbor as yourself” (Luke 10:25-37), and “That you have done it to the least of these you have done it unto me“ (Matthew 25: 31-46).

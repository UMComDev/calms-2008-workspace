Delayed Retirement of Bishops (80085-SU-¶409.1)

Amend ¶409.1 as follows:
  
¶409.1. Mandatory Retirement—a) A bishop shall be retired on August 31 next following the regular session of the jurisdictional conference if the bishop's sixty-sixth birthday has been reached on or before July 1 of the year in which the jurisdictional conference is held. However, upon the request of a bishop who would be required to retire under this provision, the jurisdictional committee on episcopacy may recommend to the jurisdictional conference a delay of one and only one quadrennium in retirement. Such a request for a delay in retirement must be presented to the committee at least one year prior to the jurisdictional conference that would immediately precede regular retirement. A recommendation for a delay in retirement for any bishop will be included in the committee’s report to the jurisdictional conference (¶50), and to be effective a delay must be approved by at least two thirds of the jurisdictional conference delegates present and voting. Any vote taken at a jurisdictional conference on a recommendation for delay in retirement of a bishop must be by anonymous ballot.

Rationale

Advances in nutrition and medicine mean that people are effective later in life, indicating that a delay in retirement is appropriate for those who are able and willing. Moreover, delaying retirement will mean a considerable saving of money to the Church.

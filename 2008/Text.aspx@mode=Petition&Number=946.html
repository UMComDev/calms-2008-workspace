Supervisory Files (80946-SU-¶423.4)

Amend ¶423.4 as follows:
  
   4. To keep and maintain appropriate supervisory records on all ministerial personnel appointed or related to charges within the district. Supervisory records shall be kept. under guidelines approved by the General Council on Finance and Administration.  Individuals in whose name a supervisory record is kept shall have access to the information contained in a record or file, with the exception of surrendered credentials and information for which a right-of-access waiver has been signed.  See ¶606.9.  Copies of materials in supervisory files that are being used against the pastor in any informal or formal complaint proceeding shall be made available to the pastor before the process shall proceed in order to fulfill  ¶362.3a). At the time of appointment change, supervisory records shall be given to the superintendent of record. The bishop shall be the superintendent of record for the district superintendents.  Anything in the file that is passed on for appointment consideration shall be made available to superintendents and pastors in question to add, correct, or challenge before it shall be used for that purpose.
   [In addition, the correction needs to be made in  ¶416.7 of the Book of Discipline.]

Rationale

These additions will clarify that the same access rights granted under Par. 606.9 are also available under Par. 423.4, thus removing some ambiguity in present church law and they will minimize abuse of the supervisory files to avoid fulfill Fair Process.

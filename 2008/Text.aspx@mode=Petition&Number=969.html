Health Care for All (80969-C2-R9999)

Add the following to The Book of Resolutions:  
The Psalms speak of the God who reminds us that it is unjust to construct or perpetuate barriers to physical wholeness or full participation in community. Every account of his ministry documents how Jesus saw restoration to health as a sign of the Kingdom of Heaven breaking in amongst us.  The Social Principles instruct us that health care, defined as a condition of physical, mental, social, and spiritual well-being, is a basic human right.  
  
Access to adequate health care is a universal right that must not be restricted by national borders or economic status.  Health care cannot, however, be pursued apart from the culture and conditions under which people live.  It is affected by beliefs, knowledge, personal habits, poverty, violence, injustice, stress and conditions in the physical environment.  
  
Therefore, we call upon all members of the global United Methodist Church to work in partnership with indigenous churches, communities and governments around the world to advocate for and help create comprehensive health care networks, including an integrated system of referral hospitals, health centers and clinics, and well-trained community health workers working together to make comprehensive health care available for all.

Support of Taiwan (80971-C1-R9999)

Add a new resolution as follows:
The human story is one of migration and the way to self determination, yet the unsolicited intervention of nations into the affairs of other nations continues to frustrate justice and that process of self-determination.  The United Methodist Church continues to stand unequivocally against such interventions.  
  
Taiwan is an island state of 23 million people, comprised of indigenous Taiwanese, Chinese and a large mixed population of both.  After 400 plus years of colonial rule by the Dutch, Chinese, & Japanese, the people of Taiwan have established a stable form of democratic government, a thriving economy, & a vibrant national identity.
  
Therefore, we call upon all United Methodists around the world to stand in solidarity with the Taiwanese people:  in support of their right of self-determination through the free voice of all the people of Taiwan without any foreign influence or domination, and  
in support of their hope of security, development, and self-determination.
We also call upon the people of The United Methodist Church, under the leadership of the Council of Bishops with the assistance of the United Methodist Women, the General Board of Global Ministries and General Board of Church and Society and their Annual Conference networks to inform themselves about the history and experience of the Taiwanese people.

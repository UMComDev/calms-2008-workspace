Deacons and Sacramental Authority (80793-MH-¶329.1)

Amend ¶329.1 as follows:
....Deacons are persons called by God, authorized by the Church and ordained by a bishop to a lifetime ministry of Word,   and   Service, and Sacrament to both the community and the congregation in a ministry that connects the two.  Ordination to sacrament will be retroactive to the first class of deacons ordained in 1997 and extended to each subsequent class of Deacons.  

Rationale

Giving Sacramental authority to deacons would extend God’s table to marginalized people in communities under-served by traditional congregations.  The New Testament provides precedent for deacons to baptize.  Philip baptized the eunuch with God’s blessing.  All people deserve access to the sacraments; not just those who worship in a congregation.

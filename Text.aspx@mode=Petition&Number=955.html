Return to the Earth (80955-GM-R9999)


Add a new resolution as follows:
RETURN TO THE EARTH

WHEREAS, in 1990 the national Native American Graves and Repatriation Act mandated the return of human remains and ritual objects to Native American tribes and nations, it further specified that proper respect be shown towards human skeletal remains, and proper interment be provided when ancestors are identified.  Presently, over 100,000 human remains still exist in various repositories, and  
  
WHEREAS, the “Return to the Earth” project of the Mennonite Central Committee has developed  a Study Guide, maintains a website, established a model cemetery (series of regional cemeteries) to receive culturally unidentifiable Native American remains, plans for construction of small cedar boxes and sewing of linen burial cloths, and
  
WHEREAS, the Bible tells significant stories through Abraham, Sarah and Joseph the importance of burial site and interment of bones (Genesis 23). Native American traditions and Christian faiths have similar views, and
  
WHEREAS, a program of this nature would provide an opportunity for the United Methodist Church to engage in its commitment for Restorative Justice  
  
Therefore, be it resolved, The United Methodist Church pledge its support to the “Return to the Earth” project in the following ways:
  
    1.    Utilize the Study Guide “Return to the Earth Project” as an educational resource

    2.    Engage in Dialogue with Native Americans in your local area
          3.    Advocate when appropriate  
          4.    Assist with construction of small cedar burial boxes and sewing of burial cloth
          5.    Monetary contributions to the project    
    

Rationale

Encourage the United Methodist Church to support the “Return to the Earth” a repatriation project initiated by the Mennonite Central Committee. This project provides an opportunity for education, dialogue and build relationships in native and non native communities.
